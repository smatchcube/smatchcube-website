Exercise 1.15

The number of times the sine procedure is applied is \(5\) because \(12.15 (1/3)^4=0.15>0.1\) and \(12.15(1/3)^5=0.05<0.1\) (the angle is divided by \(3\) each time the function is applied). The number of times the sine procedure is applied on an angle \(a\) is the integer \(n\) such that \(a (1/3)^n < 0.1\) and the solution is the logarithm of \(0.1/a\) in base \(1/3\) (the answer is the ceiling of that logarithm to be exact). So this procedure has an order of growth of \((_{1/3}{(3)} )\) in time if I'm not wrong.

As explain later in the book, it's quite useless to have a really precise idea of the order of growth so we can simply say that the order of growth in time of the sine procedure is \(((n))\). The order of growth in space is \((n)\).
