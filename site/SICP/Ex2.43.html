This question is not trivial. My analyze may not be 100% false though, queen-cols is called too many times.

The original queens procedure calls queen-cols only \(n+1\) times. Louis's procedure calls the queen-cols procedure for both each new-row and each column, hence for a board of size \(n\) the procedure queen-cols is called \(n^n\) times in contrary to only \(n+1\) times from the original procedure.\ So if the original procedure solves the puzzle in time \(T\), Louis's procedure may solves the puzzle in approximately time \(T^T\).
